About Us

Welcome to the Department of Biostatistics at Coffee University, where statistical rigor meets biological innovation. Our department has been at the forefront of quantitative research since 1985, developing cutting-edge methodologies that advance public health, medical research, and biological sciences.

We are committed to training the next generation of biostatisticians through rigorous academic programs, collaborative research opportunities, and real-world applications. Our faculty members are internationally recognized experts who contribute to both methodological innovation and impactful applied research.

Research Areas

Clinical Trials

Developing and applying statistical methods for the design and analysis of clinical trials, including randomized controlled trials, adaptive designs, and sequential monitoring. Research in this area supports rigorous evaluation of medical interventions and evidence-based decision-making in clinical research.

Genomics & Bioinformatics

Advancing statistical methodologies for the analysis of high-dimensional biological data, including gene expression, genomic sequencing, and integrative multi-omics studies. This area emphasizes scalable modeling approaches and reproducible analysis for complex biological systems.

Epidemiology

Developing statistical models for population-based health research, disease surveillance, and causal inference. Faculty research addresses challenges in observational data analysis, confounding adjustment, and longitudinal population studies to inform public health policy.

Survival Analysis

Creating and applying advanced methods for time-to-event data, including competing risks, recurrent events, and dynamic risk prediction. This research supports clinical and epidemiological studies involving long-term follow-up and censored outcomes.

Machine Learning

Applying modern machine learning and reinforcement learning methods to biomedical and health data, with a focus on interpretability, robustness, and decision-making under uncertainty. Research includes predictive modeling, representation learning, and sequential decision processes in healthcare applications.

Environmental Health

Developing statistical approaches for assessing environmental exposures and their impacts on human health. Research focuses on exposure modeling, risk assessment, and the integration of environmental and health outcome data.

Academic Programs

Duration: 2 years

Duration: 5 years

The doctoral program trains independent researchers through advanced coursework, qualifying examinations, and original dissertation research under expert mentorship.

Graduate Certificate

Duration: 1 year

A flexible certificate program for professionals seeking specialized training in biostatistical methods and data analysis.

Admissions & Application Prerequisites

Our graduate programs are designed for students who are ready for rigorous quantitative training and applied work in the health sciences. We review applications holistically, but applicants are expected to have a strong foundation in mathematics, statistics, and computing.

Application window: December 1–31.

Minimum Preparation by Program

MS in Biostatistics

Recommended background:

  • Bachelor's degree in a quantitative field (e.g., statistics, mathematics, engineering, computer science, economics, biology/health sciences with substantial quantitative coursework).
  • Calculus sequence (multivariable strongly recommended).
  • Linear algebra (proof-based course helpful but not required).
  • Intro probability and mathematical statistics (or equivalent preparation).
  • Programming experience in R or Python (prior data analysis projects preferred).
  • Three letters of recommendation.
  • TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo English Test required for applicants whose first language is not English (may be waived for some applicants).

PhD in Biostatistics

Recommended background:

  • Bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in a quantitative field (or substantial quantitative coursework).
  • Strong prior training in probability and mathematical statistics.
  • Linear algebra and advanced calculus; exposure to real analysis is beneficial.
  • Evidence of research potential (e.g., thesis, publications, substantive projects, or research experience).
  • Comfort with programming and reproducible workflows (R/Python; version control encouraged).
  • Statement of purpose.
  • Three letters of recommendation.
  • TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo English Test required for applicants whose first language is not English (may be waived for some applicants).
